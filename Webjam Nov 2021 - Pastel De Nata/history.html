Humble Beginnings

The pastel de nata has a long history, which starts in 18th century Belem. The monks of the Jeronimos Monestary would use egg whites to starch their clothes. This left them with plenty of egg yolks with which they would create pastes to sell to generate revenew for the monestary.

When the monestary closed in 1834, they sold the recpie ot the owners of the Fabrica de Pasteis de Belem. Today you can taste the recipe that has been a closely guarded secret since.
